Research question: What are the cognitive effects of chronic sleep deprivation?

--- SOURCE S1: Van Dongen et al. (2003) ---

Title: The Cumulative Cost of Additional Wakefulness: Dose-Response Effects on
Neurobehavioral Functions and Sleep Physiology From Chronic Sleep Restriction
and Total Sleep Deprivation

Published in: Sleep, Vol. 26, No. 2

Abstract excerpt:
Chronic restriction of sleep to 6 hours or less per night produced cognitive
performance deficits equivalent to up to 2 nights of total sleep deprivation.
Sleepiness ratings suggested that subjects were largely unaware of these
increasing deficits, which were reflected on a psychomotor vigilance task (PVT)
and the Karolinska Sleepiness Scale (KSS).

Key findings:
- 14 days of sleep restriction to 6h produced deficits matching 2 nights
  of total deprivation
- Subjects failed to recognize their own impairment — subjective sleepiness
  stabilized while objective performance continued to decline
- Psychomotor vigilance task (PVT) showed the most consistent impairment
- Working memory and cognitive throughput were significantly affected
- Effect was dose-dependent: 4h restriction worse than 6h, 6h worse than 8h

Study design: Controlled laboratory study, 48 subjects across three sleep
restriction conditions (4h, 6h, 8h per night) over 14 days.

Authors: Hans P.A. Van Dongen, Greg Maislin, Janet M. Mullington, David F. Dinges
Institution: University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine
