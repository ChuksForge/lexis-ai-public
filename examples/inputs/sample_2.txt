Research question: Does remote work improve productivity for knowledge workers?
Analytical lens: Focus on knowledge worker output quality, not just quantity.

--- SOURCE S1: Bloom et al. (2015) ---

Title: Does Working from Home Work? Evidence from a Chinese Experiment
Published in: Quarterly Journal of Economics

Key findings:
- Remote workers showed a 13% performance increase compared to office workers
- Performance gains attributed to: fewer breaks, fewer sick days, quieter
  working environment, no commute fatigue
- Measured in a call centre context — output metric: calls handled per shift
- Employees reported higher job satisfaction under remote arrangement
- Attrition fell 50% among remote workers during the study period

Study design: Randomized controlled trial. 249 employees at Ctrip (Chinese
travel agency). 9-month study. Call centre workers with easily quantifiable
output.

Note from authors: These results may not generalize to all knowledge work
contexts. Call centre output is highly measurable — creative and collaborative
work may respond differently.

--- SOURCE S2: Microsoft Work Trend Index (2022) ---

Title: Great Expectations: Making Hybrid Work Work
Published by: Microsoft Corporation

Key findings:
- Analysis of communication patterns across millions of Microsoft 365 users
- Remote work caused professional networks to become more siloed over time
- Connections between teams weakened; within-team ties strengthened
- The average Teams user sends 45% more chats after 6pm than pre-pandemic
- Lack of autonomy and unclear expectations were top two burnout predictors
  among knowledge workers specifically
- Innovation and tacit knowledge transfer suffer in fully remote settings
- Junior employees lose informal learning opportunities without in-person access
  to senior colleagues

Methodology: Behavioral data analysis from Microsoft 365 platform usage,
supplemented by survey of 31,000 workers across 31 countries.

Note: This is an industry-funded report. Microsoft has commercial interests in
hybrid work infrastructure. Results should be interpreted with this in mind.
